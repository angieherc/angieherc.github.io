About

Me

My journey with yoga started in 2012, during this time I've had the opportunity to practice and participate in different styles of yoga.

I started to practice yoga as physical activity and I've stayed here, because of the discipline, serenity, and empathy that brings day by day to my life.

Styles

— Ashtanga & Rocket Yoga Style

Rocket and Ashtanga Yoga are an invigorating and dynamic style of yoga that offers numerous benefits and unique challenges.

  • Strength and Flexibility: The fast-paced sequences and challenging poses in these styles of Yoga help build both strength and flexibility, enhancing overall physical fitness.
  • Energy Boost: The practice's dynamic flow and emphasis on inversions can provide an energizing effect, leaving practitioners feeling revitalized and uplifted.
  • Improved Balance and Focus: Rocket Yoga's focus on balancing poses and continuous movement promotes enhanced concentration and mental clarity.
Overall, Ahstanga and Rocket Yoga are an exhilarating style that offers a range of benefits while encouraging practitioners to embrace challenges, learn from them, and evolve in their yoga journey.

— Vinyasa, Pilates Remix Style

Mixing Vinyasa yoga postures with Pilates offers a holistic approach to physical fitness and well-being.

  • Total Body Workout: The combination of Vinyasa yoga's flowing sequences and Pilates' core-focused exercises provides a comprehensive full-body workout, targeting strength, flexibility, and balance.
  • Body Awareness: The combination of Vinyasa yoga and Pilates encourages body awareness, leading to better alignment and posture in daily activities.
  • Balanced Musculature: Integrating Vinyasa yoga and Pilates helps promote balanced muscle development, preventing overuse or strain in specific areas.
by merging Vinyasa yoga postures with Pilates, practitioners can enjoy a well-rounded, transformative practice that nurtures physical strength, flexibility, and mental clarity.

Yoga around the world.

I have taken yoga classes in different languages. Spanish of course, English, Italian, French and German. Well, changing languages is always challenging, but undoubtedly the first time I took a yoga class in German was another level. Of course there are many poses that use the name in Sanskrit or even in English, but there is something in the tone that is different and a bit scary to tell the truth.

Yoga Goals?

"The yoga pose is not the goal. Becoming flexible is not the goal. Standing on your hands is not the goal. The goal is to create space where you were once stuck. To unveil the layers of protection you have built around your heart. To appreciate your body and become aware of the mind and the noise it creates. To make peace with who you are. The goal is to love, well… Yourself, your journey, your present."

- Rachel Brathen - One of my favs

Quick workout - 300 reps
